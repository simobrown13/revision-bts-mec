Hello, I am Professor Alan Wright from the University of Manchester, and today I would like to talk about sustainability in the construction industry. The built environment is responsible for approximately forty percent of global carbon emissions. This includes both embodied carbon, which comes from manufacturing and transporting building materials, and operational carbon, which relates to energy consumption during the building lifetime. Many developers are now specifying low carbon concrete, which replaces a portion of the cement with ground granulated blast furnace slag or fly ash. This can reduce the carbon footprint of concrete by up to fifty percent. Timber construction is also gaining popularity, particularly cross laminated timber, known as C L T, which is being used for buildings up to eighteen storeys high. Another important trend is the adoption of passive house standards, which dramatically reduce energy consumption through superior insulation, airtight construction, and mechanical ventilation with heat recovery. In France, the R E 2020 regulation has set ambitious targets for reducing both the energy consumption and the carbon footprint of new buildings. The key message is that sustainability is no longer optional. It is becoming a fundamental requirement in every construction project.
