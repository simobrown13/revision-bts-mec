Thank you all for joining this meeting. I am Sarah Chen, BIM coordinator for the Morrison Tower project. I want to discuss how we are implementing Building Information Modelling on this scheme. As you know, the client has required a full BIM Level Two approach. This means all stakeholders, including the architect, structural engineer, and quantity surveyor, must work collaboratively using a shared digital model. We have chosen Revit as our primary modelling tool, and we are using a common data environment to manage document exchange. The main challenge so far has been coordination between disciplines. We have detected over two hundred clashes in the latest model, mostly between the mechanical services and the structural beams. The clash detection process has actually saved us a significant amount of time and money compared to finding these issues on site. In terms of quantities, we are now extracting bill of quantities directly from the model, which has reduced estimation time by approximately thirty percent. Looking ahead, we plan to integrate four D scheduling by linking the model to our construction programme in Navisworks. This will allow us to visualise the build sequence and identify potential delays before they happen.
